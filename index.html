Spaghetti Bolognese

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 mushrooms, finely diced
  • 1 carrot (or stick of celery/capsicum), finely diced
  • 4 Tbs olive oil (increase if dairy-free)
  • 2 Tbs butter (optional)
  • 300g mince (vegetarian)
  • 150ml red wine/red wine vinegar
  • pinch of sugar
  • 700ml jar passata/800g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 400ml water
  • 2 tsp balsamic reduction
  • 5 springs thyme, tough stalks removed
  • 1 sprig sage, leaves roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 small stalk of fresh rosemary, stripped and leaves roughly chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 30g pine nuts (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cracked black pepper
  • 400g dried spaghetti
  • 150g cheese (optional)

Method

  1. Gather and prepare your ingredients
  2. Put the olive oil and the butter in a pot and heat it together
  3. One high heat, add the onion, garlic, mushrooms and carrot to fry until they start to colour
  4. Fry for a couple of minutes. Then add the wine
  5. Stir through and reduce the heat for 1 minute. Now add the jar passata, water, balsamic reduction, sprigs fresh thyme, sprig sage, oregano, rosemary, bay leaves and the pine nuts
  6. Simmer for 25 minutes, stirring occasionally. When thickened, season it with the salt and black pepper
  7. Fill another pot 3/4 full with water, cover it with a lid and bring it to boil. Afterwards at a fitting amount of salt and the spaghetti. Stir, then boil for 10 minutes or until just cooked through. Drain through a colander and put back in the pot (not on the heat though), add the sauce and mix through gently
  8. Grate the cheese to add to the pasta

Chocolate Brownies

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup All-purpose flour
  • 3/4 Cup Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 Cup Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp Baking powder
  • 1/4 salt
  • 1/2 Unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 Cup Marple sirup
  • 1/4 Cup (plant-)milk
  • 1/4 Cup Vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1/2 Cup (Non-dairy) semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 Cup Coarsely chopped toasted walnuts

Method

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C, place rack in the middle; coat a 20cm square baking dish with vegetable oil; set aside
  2. Place the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl and whisk to aerate and combine
  3. Place the applesauce, maple syrup, plant based milk, oil, and vanilla in a large bowl and whisk to combine
  4. Add the flour mixture and fold with a rubber spatula until just combined (do not over mix)
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips and walnuts
  6. Scrape the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth out the top
  7. Bake in the oven for about 35 mins or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean
  8. Place the dish on a wire rack to cool for at least 30 minutes before serving
  9. If not serving right away, cool completely, cover tightly and store at room temperature. These brownies taste best if eaten within a day or two

Vegetable Pasta Bake

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 300g dried penne pasta
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large aubergine, chopped
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 peppers, deseeded and finely sliced
  • 2 courgettes, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 tbsp tomato purée
  • small bunch of basil, leaves picked and stems finely chopped
  • 400g can cherry tomatoes
  • 25g hard cheese, grated
  • 25g grated mozzarella
  • 75g mature cheddar, grated
  • 20g panko breadcrumbs

Method

  1. Cook the pasta following pack instructions, reserving a mugful of the pasta water and draining it 2 mins before the stated time
  2. Heat 2 tbsp of the oil in a deep, non-stick pan over a low-medium heat, then tip in the aubergine. Stir well to coat in the oil, frying until softened and deeply golden all over, around 10-15 mins. Remove it to a bowl, but exclude the remaining oil
  3. Pour the remaining oil into the pan. Once hot, tip in the onion with a pinch of salt and fry for 5-7 mins until just softened before stirring in the peppers and courgettes
  4. Cook for a further 15-17 mins until lightly browned then add the garlic. Cook for a couple of minutes until fragrant
  5. Stir in the tomato purée and allow it to caramelise for 5 mins
  6. Pour in the cooked aubergine, tomatoes, basil stems and half of the basil leaves. Bring to a gentle simmer. Leave to bubble away for 20-25 mins, then season to taste
  7. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6
  8. Mix in the cooked pasta along with a generous splash of the cooking water. Season the mixture well to taste and tip into a baking dish
  9. Combine the cheeses with the panko and a large pinch of black pepper, then scatter this over the pasta
  10. If cooking straightaway, bake for 15-20 mins until bubbling and golden on top

    11. (Will keep frozen, well covered, for up to three months. Defrost thoroughly before cooking, or cook from frozen covered in foil for 40 mins, then uncovered for a further 20 mins until piping hot throughout)

  11. Scatter over the remaining basil leaves to serve

Italian Pasta Salad

Ingredients

  • 300 g small pasta of choice

  • ½ c chopped fresh basil
  • ½ cucumber

Method

Bean Chilli

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 400g can beans (black beans, kidney beans or borlotti)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seed (or ground cumin)
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon chilli powder or chilli paste
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced or diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced or chopped finely
  • Capsicum (or vegetable of your choice)
  • 1 can chopped or whole tomatoes (400g)
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Method

  1. Heat a medium-sized heavy saucepan (don’t add any oil) and toast the cumin, oregano, paprika and chilli powder on a medium heat until fragrant. Stir constantly
  2. Add onion, garlic and chilli paste to the pan and stir to coat with the herbs and spices
  3. Drain the juice from the can of tomatoes into the pot and let that simmer with the lid on for about 10 minutes
  4. Add the can of drained beans, the tomatoes and capsicum, then simmer with the lid on for 20 to 30 minutes
  5. Taste for seasoning before you add the salt, it may not be necessary, and add liquid smoke for some variation

Sausage and Mash with Gravy

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • (Vegan) sausages
  • Potatoes mashed with (vegan) butter/spread
  • Plain (soy) milk
  • Salt and pepper
  • Peas, carrots, broccoli (or vegetables of your choice)
  • Tomato sauce
  • 3 Tbsp (vegan) butter
  • 1 brown onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbsp miso paste
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce/tamari sauce
  • ½ tsp of dried thyme
  • 2 Tbsp plain flour/ GF flour
  • 2 cups of plant-based chicken stock or vegetable stock

Method

  1. Boil potatoes. Once cooked mash with vegan butter of your choice, a splash of soy milk, salt and pepper
  2. Cook sausages according to instructions
  3. Cook peas, or any other veggies
  4. Prepare the ingredients: dice the onion and mince the garlic
  5. In a medium saucepan, melt vegan butter on medium heat and add onion and garlic. Sauté for 3-4 minutes, until fragrant
  6. Add miso paste, soy/tamari sauce and dried thyme. Mix well and fry for 2 minutes
  7. Incorporate flour and fry for 2-3 more minutes
  8. Pour in approx. 1/3 of the stock and whisk until well combined. Add the remaining stock and mix again
  9. Bring to a boil and then reduce to medium-low heat. Simmer for about 10 minutes
  10. Add salt and pepper to taste
  11. This gravy can be served chunky, or if you prefer a smooth gravy, you can strain it through a sieve

Sausage Rolls

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 large potato,peeled, boiled and mashed
  • 1 pack of soy protein mince 300g
  • Mixed herbs
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 x puff pastry sheets (vegan)
  • Some (plant-based) mylk for brushing

Method

  1. Fry onion until soft
  2. Place the soy mince, onion, mashed potato with salt, and herbs into a bowl. Combine well

Burger Patties

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 can or 1 ½ cups cooked and drained kidney beans
  • 1 can or 1 ½ cups cooked and drained black beans
  • 1 Tbsp of parsley
  • 1 tsp soy or tamari sauce
  • 1 tsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 Tbsp wholemeal flour
  • ½ cup of flour to coat the patties
  • A pinch of salt
  • 3-4 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil/avocado oil to fry

Method

  1. Prepare the ingredients. Dice the onion, mince the garlic, and chop the parsley
  2. In a medium sized pan, warm the oil over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, salt, chili, cumin, and coriander powders. Sauté for 5 minutes, until golden and fragrant
  3. In a food processor, place beans, spiced onion and garlic mixture, parsley, soy sauce, red wine, vinegar, olive oil, and wholemeal flour. Blitz until all the beans are broken down and the mixture is well combined
  4. Transfer the mixture to a container. Cover with a lid and refrigerate for at least 1 hour
  5. In a large, shallow, bowl, mix flour and salt. Warm the oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat
  6. Remove the minced beans from the fridge and, using your hands, shape a large ball (use approx.6th of the amount). Press the ball to shape a patty. Place the patty in the bowl with the flour and flip to evenly coat
  7. Transfer the patty straight into the well heated pan and fry on each side for approx. 4 minutes, until brown and crispy. Repeat with the remaining minced beans

Spinach & Ricotta Cannelloni

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 2.5 cups cashews, soaked in boiling water for 10 minutes
  • 200 ml water
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 150 g spinach, wilted
  • 1 box cannelloni
  • 2 cans chopped tomatoes
  • ½ onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • (Vegan) cheese

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees Celsius
  2. Add filling ingredients (except wilted spinach) to a high speed blender and blend until super smooth and creamy. Mix in the spinach and place mix in a piping bag
  3. Stand cannelloni upwards and pipe mix into each tube. Place filled tubes in a baking dish (can double layer)
  4. To make the sauce, fry the onion and garlic until soft then add tinned tomatoes, spices and maple syrup. Cook for about 5 minutes
  5. Pour over cannelloni, making sure each tube is well covered. If you want to, you can sprinkle some cheese over the top
  6. Cook for 20-25 minutes until pasta is soft (poke a knife through to check this) then serve topped with cheese

Loaded Nachos

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1 Brown Onion
  • ½ med Carrot
  • 1 sml Courgette
  • ½ cup White Button Mushroom
  • ½ sml Yellow Capsicum
  • ¾ can Black Beans
  • 1 can Canned Diced Tomato
  • 1 Tbsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 Tbsp Ground Paprika
  • 1 Tbsp Ground Coriander
  • ½ tsp Chilli Flakes
  • 3 Tbsp Tomato & Capsicum Salsa (Mild)
  • 1 pkt Plain Corn Chips
  • 1 cup (Dairy Free) Grated Cheese
  • 1 handful Fresh Coriander

Method

  1. To prepare, dice onion and capsicum. Grate mushroom, courgette and carrot. Set aside
  2. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over a medium heat. Add prepared vegetables and cook, stirring, for 5 minutes or until softened
  3. Stir in drained and rinsed black beans, canned tomatoes and ground herbs and spices. Add salt as desired. Stir until well combined and simmer for 5-10 minutes
  4. Serve nacho bean mix on corn chips. Garnish with salsa, fresh coriander and grated cheese
  5. Hot Tip: Melt the cheese before adding salsa and fresh coriander for melted goodness

Pesto risotto with roasted courgette

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 cup loosely packed basil leaves
  • ¼ cup walnuts
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 225g courgette sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 3 cup vegetable broth

Method

  1. Combine the basil, walnuts, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and salt in a food processor and pulse until nearly smooth
  2. Preheat the oven to 220C
  3. Toss courgette with 1 tbsp oil and a dash of salt and pepper. Roast for about 10 minutes
  4. Warm the vegetable broth in a saucepan
  5. In a medium soup pot, sauté the onion in 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat until translucent
  6. Add the rice and stir
  7. Add the white wine and stir occasionally, until wine is mostly absorbed. Turn the heat down to medium-low
  8. Add the broth by the cupful, stirring the risotto until the broth is mostly absorbed (6 to 8 minutes)
  9. With your last addition, add the pesto. Season with salt and pepper, and serve topped with courgette

Tiramisu

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • Lady Fingers
  • ¼ cup aquafaba
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • ¼ cup caster sugar
  • ½ cup flour
  • 1 Tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Pinch salt
  • Icing sugar for dusting
  • 200-240 g Cream Cheese, room temperature
  • 300 g silken tofu
  • ⅔ cup icing sugar
  • ⅓ cup plant milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ cup strong coffee
  • 2 Tbsp brandy
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 170 degrees Celsius
  2. For the lady fingers, beat the aquafaba until stiff peaks form or until you can hold the bowl upside down without it moving. Add vanilla and caster sugar and beat until combined
  3. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking powder, cornflour and salt then sift into the aquafaba mixture. Use a spatula to fold the mixture gently together until just combined
  4. Spoon mixture into a piping bag and line a baking tray with baking paper. Pipe your ‘fingers’ onto the baking tray leaving at least a 1cm gap in between each. I made my lady fingers about 2cm by 8cm, but it’s not necessary to be exact. Dust lightly with icing sugar then bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes and allow to cool
  5. For the mascarpone cream, combine all ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth
  6. For assembly, combine coffee and brandy together in a bowl. One by one, soak each lady finger (be sure to soak well) in the coffee mixture and line the base of a dish with the soaked lady fingers. Pour over the cream mixture and spread evenly. Leave in the fridge overnight then right before serving, dust with cocoa powder

Cheese Ball

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 2 x 240 g (plant-based) cream cheese, room temperature
  • 6 - 8 pieces of crystallised ginger (c. 50 g)
  • 3 small sweet gherkins (c. 40 g)
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries or other dried fruit
  • 227 g tin of crushed pineapple in juice
  • 70 g chopped peanuts
  • 2 Tbsp sherry/brandy/juice from the can of pineapple

Method

  1. Grate the cream cheese (preferable) or cut into small pieces (next best option!)
  2. Chop ginger, gherkins and dried fruit finely
  3. Drain the pineapple, squeezing to remove as much juice as possible
  4. Mix all ingredients except the chopped peanuts in a large bowl
  5. Place the chopped peanuts in a large, shallow bowl and set aside
  6. With clean hands mix everything together. If the cream cheese is too cold this will be a bit tricky, so leave it on the bench to come to room temperature
  7. When everything is well mixed, form into as round a ball as possible
  8. Roll the ball in the chopped nuts until it’s completely covered
  9. Place on a serving plate if you’re using it straight away, or store in a covered container in the fridge (wrap in cling film if you don’t have a suitable sized container)
  10. Remove from the fridge an hour before serving

Chocolate Orange Tart

Read the recipe...

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cup almonds
  • 3 Tbsp cacao powder
  • ½ tsp vanilla powder
  • Pinch sea salt
  • ¾ cup medjoul dates, pitted
  • 1 Tbsp cacao butter, melted
  • 1 ½ cup cashews soaked 2 hrs, rinsed & drained
  • 1 cup orange segments
  • 1 Tbsp orange rind
  • ¼ cup coconut nectar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp sea salt
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ¼ cup cacao butter, melted
  • ⅓ cup cacao powder

Method

  1. Line a 22 cm tart tin (one with a removable bottom) with a layer of biodegradable food wrap (like plastic wrap, but biodegradable)
  2. Blend dry base ingredients (except dates and cacao butter) in a food processor until crumbly. Then add dates one by one while the processor is running on low, until the mixture becomes sticky. Finally pour in the melted cacao butter and pulse to combine
  3. Press into your tart tin, using your hands to push the mixture into all the grooves on the sides of the tin. It should be ~½ cm thick on the base and sides. Pop it in the freezer to set
  4. Blend all your wet ingredients except the coconut oil, cacao butter and cacao powder until silky smooth
  5. Then add in the melted coconut oil and cacao butter until completely blended, and finally add the cacao powder. Pour into your base and put the tart in the fridge overnight to set
  6. Dust with extra cacao powder and decorate with extra orange rind if desired (only add the orange rind just before serving, otherwise it will get dry)
  7. Serve with fresh orange segments and coconut yoghurt
